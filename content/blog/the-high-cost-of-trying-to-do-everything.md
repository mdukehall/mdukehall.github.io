# The High Cost of Trying to Do Everything

I work with brilliant SMB executives daily. The common thread? They are masters of the **greenfield sell**. They are hungry, agile, and focused on revenue.

But I see the same look in their eyes when we talk about the intersection of AI innovation, cloud infrastructure, and economic alignment. It's the **Priority Paradox**. They know these technologies should be optimized, but they have to choose between keeping the engine running at top speed today or rebuilding the engine for tomorrow.

## The perspective shift

You don't need to be an expert in everything. You need to be a master of **vision-led prioritization**.

- **Stop** viewing cloud economics as a chore. It's the fuel for your next GTM expansion.
- **Stop** viewing AI integration as a feature. It's the multiplier for your existing team's output.
- **Start** viewing your stack as a strategic partner—not a pile of obligations waiting for a free afternoon that never comes.

## What actually wins

The future isn't about being the smartest tech-stack optimizer in the room. It's about being the executive who can clearly articulate **why specific trade-offs are being made today** to win the market tomorrow.

That's the job. Not knowing every lever—knowing which levers matter *right now*, and having the conviction to leave the rest alone until they do.
